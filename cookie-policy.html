Cookie Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how GoViral Ads ("we," "us," "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on goviralads.agency (the "Site"). It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

01

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site function, remember your preferences, and understand how visitors interact with content. Similar technologies, such as pixels and local storage, serve related purposes.

02

Categories Of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies. Required for the Site to function properly, including navigation, form submissions, and security. These cannot be disabled without affecting core functionality.
Analytics Cookies. Help us understand how visitors use the Site, such as which pages are visited and how long visitors stay, so we can improve content and performance.
Advertising Cookies. Used by Google Ads and the Meta (Facebook and Instagram) Pixel to measure ad performance, build audiences, and deliver more relevant advertising across platforms.
03

Google & Meta Pixels

We use the Google Ads tag, Google Analytics, and the Meta Pixel to measure the performance of our advertising campaigns and understand how visitors reach our Site. These tools may set cookies that track your activity on our Site and, in some cases, across other websites, in order to build advertising audiences and report on conversions such as form submissions.

04

How To Control Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies:

  • Adjust your browser settings to block or delete cookies. Most browsers let you do this in their privacy or security settings.
  • Opt out of Google's personalized advertising through Google's Ad Settings at adssettings.google.com.
  • Opt out of Meta's personalized advertising through your Facebook or Instagram ad preferences.
  • Use browser extensions or device-level settings such as "Limit Ad Tracking" to reduce cross-site tracking.

Disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of the Site, including your ability to submit forms or navigate certain features.

05

Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our Site are placed by third parties, such as Google and Meta, whose services are embedded on our pages. We do not control these cookies directly. Please review the privacy policies of these third parties for more information about how they use cookies and your data.

06

How Long Cookies Last

Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies, including most analytics and advertising cookies, remain on your device for a set period, ranging from a few days to up to two years, depending on the provider, unless you delete them sooner. Advertising platforms such as Google and Meta set their own retention windows for the audiences and conversion data built from these cookies, which we do not control.

07

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" setting. Because there is no common industry standard for how websites should respond to these signals, our Site does not currently change its behavior when it detects a Do Not Track request. You can still manage tracking through the cookie and ad-preference controls described above.

08

Changes To This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, regulation, or our own practices. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. We encourage you to review this page periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies.
